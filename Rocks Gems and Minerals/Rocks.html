Clay

Clay is a fine-grained natural sediment, soil or rock component that comprises mostly one or more clay minerals or clays normally with trace to major amounts of quartz, iron and aluminium oxy-hydroxides and organic matter.

Limestone

Limestone, sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3), usually in the form of calcite or aragonite. It may contain considerable amounts of magnesium carbonate (dolomite) as well; minor constituents also commonly present include clay, iron carbonate, feldspar, pyrite, and quartz.

Puddingstone

Puddingstone is a popular name applied to a conglomerate that consists of distinctly rounded pebbles whose colors contrast sharply with the color of the finer-grained, often sandy, matrix or cement surrounding them.

Slate

Usually gray to black, sometimes green, yellow, brown, or red. Slates are composed mostly of quartz, micas, chlorite, with minor hematite, clays, and other minerals.

Obsidian


Glassy, fresh igneous rocks with a high silica content and conchoidal fracture. The earliest manufactured mirrors were pieces of polished obsidian.
